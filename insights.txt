## Key Insights

### 1. Customer Segmentation
Students and retired customers have more than double the average conversion rate, but their small share (2–4%) limits overall impact.  
Blue-collar customers have the lowest conversion rate (~6.9%) despite forming nearly 20% of the customer base.

### 2. Campaign Effectiveness
Conversion remains stable (10–13%) for 1–3 contacts but declines with additional calls, indicating customer fatigue.

### 3. Call Duration Impact
Calls longer than 5 minutes achieve ~27% conversion compared to ~7% for shorter calls.

### 4. Previous Campaign Outcome
Customers with previous successful outcomes show the highest likelihood of conversion.

### 5. Economic Influence
Lower interest rates are associated with higher conversion rates, while higher rates reduce conversion significantly.

### 6. Combined Insight
Students with longer calls show ~50% conversion but should be targeted selectively due to cost and size.


## Recommendations

- Focus on high-conversion segments  
- Avoid excessive follow-ups  
- Prioritize longer, meaningful calls  
- Adjust strategy based on economic conditions  
- Improve targeting for low-performing segments
